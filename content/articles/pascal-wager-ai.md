AI is changing the job market.
Entire categories of work are getting compressed or automated: customer service, internal operations, entry-level software tasks, and even content creation.

But there is one category that isn’t being commoditized. In fact, it is growing in leverage and impact.

People who can build.

## The Builder Advantage

Not just developers in the narrow sense, but anyone who can combine systems, tools, and code to create real things. Real, working solutions for real people.
That means building products end-to-end, solving problems with automation, and using AI to go from idea to impact.
While most jobs are being assisted or replaced by AI, builders are being amplified. You can go from idea to prototype in hours. One person can now do what once took a full team.


There are many examples of this happening over the past year...
- Cursor: Two-person team built an AI-powered code editor challenging incumbents like VS Code and Copilot.
- Harvey: Started by a small group, Harvey became the go-to AI legal assistant for top law firms around the world.
- Interview Coder/Cluely: Created by students at Columbia, it went viral as an AI tool for coding interviews, hit $3M ARR, and raised $5.3M.

All of these have in common that they were started by a small team and were able to grow exponentially.
These aren’t one-offs; they are still-early signs of what is possible when builders embrace leverage.

## Why AI Is Better for Builders
Everyone gets a productivity boost from AI. But what you do with that boost makes all the difference.
If your job is to complete tasks, AI helps you do them faster, which is a linear speed up.
You are still doing one thing at a time, just more quickly.

But if your job is to build systems, AI speeds up your whole loop: building, testing, automating, improving. You’re not just doing things faster—you’re creating systems that do things for you, again and again.

For example,
- A marketer uses AI to write emails 2x faster. Helpful.
- A builder uses AI to create an automated campaign engine that writes, personalizes, and sends emails while they sleep. Now every future campaign costs zero time—and scales.

That’s the shift. The task-doer gets a time boost but the builder gets a leverage engine.

## Why This Matters Now
Most people will use AI the way they use Excel or Google Docs—as a productivity tool, nothing more.

But the people who build with AI—who turn it into products, systems, workflows—will shape the future.

You don’t need to be a machine learning expert. You just need to:
- Write some code
- Understand APIs
- Learn how to integrate tools
- Ship things
- Use AI to go faster

The floor has been raised. But so has the ceiling—for those who can reach it.

## Pascal’s Wager

The downside of learning to build is low. It is a useful skill even in a slow-moving world.
But the upside is huge—it's the best shot at staying relevant, independent, and in control if AI does transform everything.

This is the core argument: building is the most rational bet in an uncertain future.


## The Wager

You don’t have to believe in AGI. You don’t need to make a doomsday bet. You just have to look at the trajectory and ask:

> What’s the smartest bet I can make, knowing that the world is uncertain—but compounding fast?

And the answer is clear: Learn to build. Use AI. Own the leverage. That’s the wager. And it’s still early.
